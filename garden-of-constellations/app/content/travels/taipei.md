# Taipei

Taipei feels like a city that never fully slows down, even at night.

There’s a contrast everywhere — modern glass towers beside quiet temples, fast-moving metro lines under streets filled with small food stalls.

---

## First impressions

The air feels humid and alive.

Street crossings are organized but still full of motion. Scooters fill intersections like flowing traffic rivers.

---

## Food culture

One of the strongest memories of Taipei is the food.

- Night markets with endless stalls  
- Bubble tea shops on almost every corner  
- Steam, smoke, and noise blending together  

Eating outside feels normal here, even late at night.

---

## Places

- Taipei 101 standing above everything like a landmark anchor  
- Small temples hidden between apartment buildings  
- Night markets like Shilin where time feels irrelevant  

---

## Mood

Taipei feels:

- energetic but not overwhelming  
- modern but still rooted in tradition  
- chaotic in a controlled way  

---

## Closing thought

Taipei doesn’t feel like a place you “finish exploring.”

It feels like a place you just keep returning to, noticing different details each time.
